## About This Course

Ayurveda holds that almost everything found in nature can be used as medicine. The tradition has over 3,500 years of recorded knowledge about medicinal plants — the first classical text, the Charaka Samhita, lists 50 groups of herbs classified by their therapeutic action, covering more than 500 individual plants.

This specialist course focuses on the most useful and accessible of those herbs — the ones well-documented by both classical texts and modern research, and increasingly available in Japan. It is taught by Professor H.S. Sharma, one of India's leading pharmacology scholars, and combines rigorous theory with hands-on herb tasting and identification sessions.

## Who Is This For?

This course is designed for those who have completed the Ayurveda Talk Course or who already have foundational Ayurveda knowledge. It is also suitable for herbalists, naturopaths, nutritionists, and healthcare professionals who want to understand the Ayurvedic approach to medicinal plants.

## Practical Sessions

Every pair of sessions includes a practical component where participants taste, smell, and examine fresh and dried herbs, prepare simple herbal formulations, and learn how to assess quality. You will leave each session with sample herbs to take home.
