Sesame oil — *tila taila* in Sanskrit — holds a unique position in Ayurvedic medicine. Of all plant oils, it is considered the most therapeutically potent, the most penetrating, and the most deeply nourishing to the nervous system and tissues.

## Why Sesame?

Ayurveda classifies sesame oil as:
- **Warming** — particularly beneficial for Vata types and in autumn/winter
- **Heavy and unctuous** — deeply nourishing to the seven tissue layers (dhatus)
- **Penetrating** — able to cross the skin barrier and reach deeper tissues
- **Stabilising** — grounding for an agitated nervous system

## Abhyanga: Self-Oil Massage

This is the most important use of sesame oil in daily practice. The classical texts recommend Abhyanga as a daily practice, describing it as conferring the same benefits as exercise: improved circulation, toned muscles, healthy skin, and enhanced resilience to stress and fatigue.

**How to do it:**
1. Warm the oil slightly by placing the bottle in a bowl of hot water for a few minutes
2. Apply to the entire body, beginning with the scalp and working downward
3. Use long strokes on the limbs, circular motions on the joints
4. Leave on for at least 10–20 minutes (or longer if time permits)
5. Shower or bathe in warm (not hot) water, using minimal soap

The oil remaining on the skin after bathing is considered therapeutically valuable — don't scrub it all off.

## Oil Pulling (Kavala)

Swishing 1–2 tablespoons of sesame oil in the mouth for 5–20 minutes upon waking is an ancient Ayurvedic oral hygiene practice. It strengthens the gums and teeth, reduces bacteria, and — according to the classical texts — benefits the entire head region.

**Important:** Always spit the oil into the bin (not the sink, to avoid plumbing issues). Never swallow.

## Choosing the Right Oil

For Abhyanga, use **cold-pressed, unrefined sesame oil** (not toasted sesame oil, which is used in cooking and is too strong-smelling for massage). Some practitioners prefer to lightly cure the oil by heating it to 100°C briefly — this is said to enhance its penetrating properties.

For Vata types: sesame oil is ideal year-round.
For Pitta types: coconut oil is often preferable in summer.
For Kapha types: lighter oils (mustard, sunflower) or dry brushing may suit better.
