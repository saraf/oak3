As winter melts into spring, Ayurveda recognises a shift in the dominant dosha: **Kapha** — the earth-and-water force of structure, stability, and coolness — has been accumulating all winter long. Now, as temperatures rise and humidity increases, that accumulation can tip into imbalance: sluggishness, congestion, weight gain, and a general sense of heaviness.

## Why Spring is the Time to Cleanse

In Ayurvedic thinking, spring is nature's own detox season. Just as snow melts and rivers run, the body naturally begins to release what it has stored. But without conscious support, this release can feel uncomfortable. Gentle cleansing practices help the body work *with* this seasonal shift rather than against it.

## Foods to Favour

- **Light, dry, and warming**: barley, millet, rye, lentils
- **Bitter greens**: dandelion, rocket, mustard greens, kale
- **Pungent spices**: ginger, black pepper, turmeric, cumin, fenugreek
- **Honey**: the one sweet that actively reduces Kapha (use raw, never heated)

## Foods to Reduce

- Heavy, oily, cold, or sweet foods
- Dairy, especially yoghurt and cheese
- Wheat and refined carbohydrates
- Cold drinks and ice

## Herbal Support

**Triphala** is the classic Ayurvedic formula for spring — it gently stimulates digestion, supports elimination, and tonifies the tissues. Taken warm with honey before bed, it prepares the body beautifully for a fresh season.

**Ginger tea** throughout the day stokes digestive fire (Agni), keeping sluggish Kapha in check.

## Daily Routine Adjustments

- Rise earlier — before 6am if possible — to avoid the heavy Kapha hours of morning
- Dry brushing (Garshana) with a raw silk glove stimulates lymphatic flow
- Exercise more vigorously than in winter: jogging, yoga with backbends and inversions
- Reduce sleep slightly — Kapha thrives on oversleeping

A conscious spring transition, guided by these principles, sets the tone for a healthy, energised year.
