Ten days in Kerala is unlike anything else. From the moment we arrived at the resort — surrounded by coconut palms, paddy fields, and the particular green that belongs only to Kerala — the pace shifted. This is what Ayurveda feels like in its natural habitat.

Each participant received an initial consultation with the resident vaidya and a personalised treatment programme. Some needed gentle rest and nourishment; others deeper cleansing work. The tradition is precise in this way — no two programmes were the same.

The herb garden visit was a highlight for many. Standing among the plants you have only read about — seeing Ashwagandha, Brahmi, and Neem growing in their native soil — makes the whole tradition feel immediate and alive.

We return in March 2026. If you are considering joining, please enquire early — places fill quickly.
